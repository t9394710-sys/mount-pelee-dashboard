About Mount Pelée

Mount Pelée is an active volcano located on the island of Martinique in the Caribbean. It is famous for its major eruption in 1902 which destroyed the city of Saint-Pierre. Scientists monitor Mount Pelée carefully because it can produce explosive eruptions, ash clouds, and pyroclastic flows.

How Scientists Monitor Volcanoes

  • Seismometers detect earthquakes underground
  • Gas sensors measure volcanic gases
  • Satellites detect heat and ash clouds
  • Ground deformation sensors detect swelling

Volcano Safety Kit

  • Clean drinking water
  • Flashlight and batteries
  • Dust masks to protect from ash
  • Emergency food supplies
  • First aid kit
  • Battery powered radio

Important Safety Tips

  • Follow evacuation instructions from authorities
  • Stay indoors during ashfall
  • Protect eyes and lungs from ash
  • Avoid river valleys during eruptions
  • Keep emergency supplies ready
